All About Brown Bears

Introduction

The Brown Bear (Ursus arctos) is a large bear species found across Eurasia and North America. They are the most widely distributed bear species and have several recognized subspecies.

Habitat

Brown bears inhabit diverse environments including forests, mountains, and tundra. They can be found in North America, Europe, and Asia.

Diet

Brown bears are omnivorous, feeding on a variety of foods. Their diet includes berries, roots, insects, fish, and mammals. They have a particular preference for salmon where available.

Quick Facts

  • Scientific Name: Ursus arctos
  • Lifespan: 20-30 years in the wild
  • Weight: Up to 600 kg (1,322 lbs)
  • Length: 1.4 to 2.8 meters (4.6 to 9.2 feet)
  • Subspecies: Grizzly bear, Kodiak bear, Eurasian brown bear, etc.

Conservation Status

Brown bears are classified as Least Concern by the IUCN, but some subspecies are threatened due to habitat loss and human conflict.
